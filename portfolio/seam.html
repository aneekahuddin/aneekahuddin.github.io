Problem: Create a resizing image application that preserves interesting parts of a photo

Our Approach

First, how do we identify how much to remove?

When resizing, you change the dimensions of a photo meaning each time you resize, you remove a column or a row.

Find pixels that have the least amount of change in color in the pictures: We calculated the “energy” of a pixel using its RGB values. Then with the pixels surrounding it, we found the change in energy in the X and Y directions using the values of the pixels in the + and - 1 directions. For edge pixels, we wrapped around the photo.

Algorithm

We debated between Dykstra’s algorithm and DP. Dykstra had complexities in the code that would make it difficult to keep track of the smallest energy among all of the rows. DP was a much more straightforward approach with little tradeoff with space/time complexity and led to more readable and easier code. We went with DP. We would attempt Dykstras if DP went over time
